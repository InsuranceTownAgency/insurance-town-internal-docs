🚨 Hard Rule — Market Submission Order
This rule applies to every single commercial quote from day one. No exceptions ever.Always follow this exact order. Never go to an outside market without completing and documenting steps 1 and 2 first.
1
🟢 Farmers First — Always
Every commercial quote starts with Farmers. Check Farmers eligibility before anything else. If Farmers can write it, it stays with Farmers. If Farmers declines, log in AZ notes: Farmers Declined plus reason. Then move to KraftLake.
2
🔵 KraftLake Second — All Direct Carriers in Dashboard
Go to KraftLake dashboard. Check all direct carriers available. Submit to the most appropriate carrier. If a KraftLake carrier declines, log carrier name, case number, and reason in AZ notes. Attach screenshot showing the case ID. If all KraftLake carriers decline, log each one before moving to step 3.
3
🟡 Outside Broker Markets — Only After 1 and 2 Exhausted
Only go to outside broker markets after Farmers and all available KraftLake carriers are attempted and declined with full documentation. Outside markets include TAPCO, IMACO, US Asia, CID, Connect, Next, Ergo. Flag to Eva before submitting to any outside market on a new risk type for the first time.

🚫 Rejection Logging — Hard Rule
Every rejection must be logged the same day it happens.No exceptions. A rejection without documentation did not happen as far as the agency is concerned.
🔵 KraftLake Rejection — Exact Steps
1
Note the carrier
Write down the exact KraftLake carrier name that declined.
2
Note the case number
Copy the exact case number from the KraftLake dashboard. No case number means no move forward.
3
Take screenshot
Screenshot the decline screen. The case ID must be visible. If case ID is not visible, take another screenshot until it is.
4
Log in AZ notes
Add to AZ submission note: KL Rejected. Carrier name. Case number. Reason if shown. Screenshot attached.
5
Attach screenshot
Attach screenshot directly to the AZ record so Eva can see it without asking.
6
Move to next carrier
Only after logging the rejection, move to the next available KraftLake carrier or outside market.

⏱ Stale Lead Cadence — Commercial
Every commercial lead has a stale clock running from the last contact date. Know this cadence. Your job is Day 30 only. Everything after that is automated or handled by others.
30
Day 30 — Your Action
Add an AZ note flagging the lead as stale. Log it in your EOD. This is your only action on stale leads. Format: "Lead stale as of [date]. Last contact [date]. No response to [number] attempts." Then stop. Do not contact the client again.
45
Day 45 — Automated
Make.com fires an automatic Slack alert to you via #ops-alerts. This is just a notification. You do not need to do anything new at Day 45 if you already logged the Day 30 note.
60
Day 60 — Automated
Make.com auto-moves the card to QNC Commercial pipeline. Zel picks it up from there for re-engagement outreach. If Zel re-engages and the lead responds, Eva qualifies the lead personally. Eva then assigns back to Pedro for a fresh quote. Your pipeline is active leads only.
🚫 Do Not Contact Stale Leads After Day 30Your role ends at the Day 30 AZ note. Zel handles re-engagement from QNC. Never reach back out to a stale lead on your own. Wait for Eva to re-assign after qualification.
